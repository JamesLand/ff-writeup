Week 10

It feels like it has been ages since I did a writeup. I think the last one was back in week seven? No, that was Justin. Week six, I guess, because this week is week 10, last week was Oron, the week before was BYE, and week seven was Justin. So yeah, four weeks ago I did a writeup. Shit, I’m probably out of practice. What comes first? Bottom of the barrel? No, let’s actually do a blurb about goddamn football for once, because this isn’t a fantasy League of Legends league (how would that even work?).

 

I think I’ll start by talking about some of the highlights of week ten, at least for me. In the noon slot, I had two games that I was keeping an eye on. First, and more briefly because I didn’t have the audio on for this one, was the Chiefs vs. the Cardinals. This game featured the Return of David Johnson, a player who has been overdue for a thirty point game, and finally got one, albeit in a loss to the Chiefs. Honestly, the Cards showed a decent amount of improvement on offense this week. The Chiefs aren’t the greatest defensive team in the league, so it doesn’t take too much to be successful against them, but the Cards were at least getting DJ the ball in the flat, which is all you need to do for him to be successful. The main thing that they still struggle with is protecting Josh Rosen long enough for him to do anything, which really hurts the passing game. Meanwhile, on the defensive side of the ball they let Tyreek Hill into the endzone a couple of times, but otherwise locked down pretty well inside the red zone. The Cardinals allowed 26 points, which is not good, but considering the types of scores the Chiefs usually put up, it’s promising. The Cardinals hope to get their third win of the season (the other two being against the 49ers) vs. the Raiders next Sunday.

In the noon slot, I was more focused on my (new this season) AFC team, The cLeveLLLLLand broWWWns, who notched their first November win since 2014, which was coincidentally also against the Atlanta Falcons (as this one was). This game, in all phases, was probably the best Browns performance this season. The offense was clicking, in both the passing and running  games. Nick Chubb had his best fantasy week, with his career-long 92 yard rushing touchdown coming very early in his career, a run which he will probably never top (and the longest run in franchise history). Baker Mayfield posted fantastic numbers as well – 17 of 20, going 13/13 on his first 13 passes for 216 yards and 3 touchdowns, one of which was a nice improvised throw to Rashard Higgins on a broken play. His passer rating today of 151.3 was the highest of any of the rookie quarterbacks for a single game so far in 2018. He’s dangerous. Browns get a much-needed win to instill confidence in their team after the dismissal of their head coach and offensive coordinator, and will face the suddenly very beatable Cincinnati Bengals next week. The Falcons? They go back home to face the Dallas Cowboys, who have already beaten one bird team this month (more on that later).

I didn’t pay attention as much to the late afternoon slot games – was meeting my folks after their week-long trip to Ireland. I did, however, cross my fingers any time the Rams got near the endzone that Gurley wouldn’t get the TD, and swore up a storm when I heard about Gordon’s 60 yarder. As of the time of writing, I’m still alive in that game, assuming that Kittle stays around his season average. Come on, New York Football Giants.

In the evening, we had the spectacle of Dallas vs. Philadelphia in Philadelphia. During this last week, I was not very hopeful about this game. Dallas had just had a piss-poor showing vs the Titans on Monday Night Football, being unable to capitalize on two early turnovers and losing the game down the stretch. We were already facing a short week, but to add to that, Philly was coming off of a BYE week, and as a result had much more time to prepare for the matchup. Dallas would be out another starter on the offensive line, left guard Connor Williams, who had admittedly not been the superstar that we were expecting he would be, but was still first on the depth chart. Of course, Travis Frederick is still out and will likely remain out for the season. On the defensive side of the ball, we were missing David Irving and Taco Charlton on the line, and Sean Lee at linebacker. I didn’t know this at the time, because I’m a Cowboys homer and didn’t look at the Philly inactives, but they were pretty banged up too. I found out at gametime that Lane Johnson, their stud RT (from Oklahoma – boomer), is still inactive. Their backfield was much less dangerous than it could have been, as Ajayi and Sproles remain out. Most notably was their secondary – Jalen Mills and Sidney Jones were both out, and midgame Ronald Darby was also injured and left the game. I didn’t know all of this at the time, and so I figured that the Eagles would probably school us.

I think the biggest early tone setter was the interception by Leighton Vander Esch (LVE, from now on, because his name is long and I’m lazy). LVE’s pick put us in the red zone, where we promptly kicked a field goal, as per last week. Well, last week we missed the field goal, but whatever. Throughout most of the first half, the defense was pretty solid. Philly didn’t really have much of a running game, and went for it on fourth down early on and was unable to convert. In contrast, Dallas faked a punt at its own 31 from fourth and 2, with a direct snap to Jeff Heath, normally at safety. That was a pretty nuts call that nobody is talking about – I don’t think anybody was expecting that. However, the buzz was that Dallas was playing like the season was on the line (it kind of still is, to be honest), so some tricks were to be expected. That one worked perfectly.

 

Dallas and Philly traded field goals and the score was 6 to 3. In the Dallas drive, Elliott hurdles a dude who is standing pretty much straight up and almost scores, which is fucking insane. If he had scored it would have been play of the year, no question. With about a minute and a half left before halftime, I thought we had a chance to get another field goal and go up 9 to 3, but that hope was quickly extinguished with a pretty big sack that put us at second and 23. That is, until we ran a pretty excellent screen call to Michael Gallup, which was well blocked and got us the first down on third and 15. From there, the drive was executed perfectly, and I felt pretty optimistic about a 13 to 3 lead at halftime, and getting the ball back right afterwards.

Coming out of halftime, we ran a naked bootleg that was immediately blown up for a nine yard sack. What the fuck, Linehan? Can we stop running that play? It never works. I’ve seen that play get blown up at least four times by different teams this season. Everybody knows it’s coming. Everyone has seen it on film. It’s apparently extremely predictable. Stop doing it. We punt and Philly goes down and kicks a field goal. We go down and miss a field goal. I miss Dan Bailey, a little. Then, the next four drives are touchdown touchdown touchdown touchdown. Ertz gets both of the Eagles touchdowns, and Ian forgets what team he’s rooting for in the middle of the game, because F A N T A S Y. Elliott gets both of the Cowboys touchdowns.

It is now 27 to 20, and the Eagles are driving. There’s three minutes and some change left in the game. Philly had been kind of getting whatever they wanted in the passing game, especially with Ertz against Awuzie/whoever was on him. They get first and ten at the Dallas 38. Philly tries a pass in the flat to Smallwood, who is tracked down by LVE. Second down, Wentz scrambles for five when we cover pretty well. Third down, after the two minute warning, was the play of the game (according to Collinsworth, and I agree). Philly tries another lateral pass to Corey Clement, and they’ve got their two blockers in position against only LVE, who is kind of out of position and about to be swallowed up. Looks like an easy first. It wasn’t, though, because LVE outruns both blockers before they can get him, and drops Clement for a five yard loss, setting up fourth and seven. It was pretty legit – the type of play you expect Sean Lee to make, but LVE filled in very nicely and did the job for him. The Eagles had no chance but to go for it on fourth down, and were stopped short on a pass to Ertz by the always solid Jeff Heath. The rest of the game was pretty uneventful, and the Cowboys go on to win 27 to 20.

This was a game that started defensively and turned into a rather entertaining NFC East bout in the second half, with the good guys coming out on top. What more could you ask for from a Cowboys Eagles matchup?

I think the best way to break this down is by tier, and then team. We’ll start with the likeliest teams to make playoffs this year, the 7 and 3 teams.

Commissioner Gordon: Sidd’s team, although losing this week, is still wildly ahead on PF compared to the next highest team, which gives him an advantage down the stretch, if he were to go on a losing streak. His last three games are as follows:

  • Jaiden
  • Thomas
  • Anthony

Jaiden’s team has revitalized itself with the return of Fournette, but I think Sidd is still the favorite in that game. Thomas’s team is injury ravaged and on bye hell this week, but not really all that good – I’d consider that a solid W for Sidd, even with Gurley on BYE that week. Anthony’s team is wildly unpredictable, so I’d say lean W but probably still a win. Even if Sidd loses to both Jaiden and Anthony, his team would only fall to 8 and 5, which would still put him in the playoffs. I think he’s most likely to go 9 and 4, putting him in good position for a BYE with tiebreaks.

Jake From Saquon: Ian’s team has these matchups down the stretch:

  • Mike
  • James
  • Jaiden

None of these games are sure victories. Ian’s schedule down the stretch is the toughest of the top tier. However,  Ian’s team has been very strong, so it wouldn’t surprise me if he did win all three. I’d say he’s likely to win two of the three – pick your favorite two. That would put him at 9 and 4, solidly in the playoffs and in contention for first round BYE. If he wins all three, he would get a first round BYE for sure.

Pittsburgh Holdouts: My team is on a pretty solid winning streak, although my score cooled down this week. My last three games are as follows:

  • Oron
  • Ian
  • Dame

My schedule is pretty easy down the stretch, with Oron and Dame as likely W’s. Versus Ian is a tossup game, as his team has been very strong lately with the exception of his BYE hell week. My most likely regular season outcome is either 9 and 4 (if I win two of the three) or 10 and 3 (if I go undefeated), which will certainly guarantee me a playoff spot and maybe a first round BYE.

Next, we have the six and four teams – teams who are currently in, but not guaranteed a playoff spot by any means.

Benny and the Fournettes: Jaiden’s upcoming schedule:

  • Sidd
  • Oron
  • Ian

Jaiden is tricky to predict, considering that he just went off this week. However, I’m gonna go ahead and guess that he loses two of his last three matchups (the ones against top tier teams Sidd and Ian). This would put him in a tough spot at 7 and 6. He needs to beat one of Ian or Sidd, which would put him at 8 and 5 assuming he takes care of Oron in week 12. This would definitely push him into the playoffs.

Taco Johnny’s: Chris has the following matchups in the last three weeks:

Mike will be a challenge as well, but I’d give Chris the advantage in that one too, considering that Cooper Kupp is out for the season, and Gronk has been out more times than not. We’ll see how he does after the BYE. I’d say Chris is most likely to get 2 of 3 wins, putting him at 8 and 5.

Dame’s Daddy: Mike’s team had a good showing this week, but lost Cooper Kupp. Upcoming matchups are:

  • Dame
  • Chris

Mike is likely to beat Damian, but will face Ian and Chris down the stretch. This week will be the first win that Mike has had in a while. I think it will be tough for Mike to get a second win down the stretch, but we will see. Most likely to finish 7 and 6, a second win would push him into the playoffs for sure

Finally, the five and five “bubble” teams.

For the bubble teams, you need to get as many wins as possible to have a playoff chance. 7 wins is possibly in the playoffs, 8 wins is basically guaranteed. That’s the overall picture, with likeliest contenders for playoffs (IMO) being Sidd, me, Ian, Chris, and then two of three for Jaiden/Mike/Reece (sorry Anthony!). That said, I will probably be totally wrong on the last few spots, because football is random and any attempts to predict anything only jinx everything. I’ll probably get scorched by Oron 150-50 next week, lose the last two games, and miss playoffs on tiebreaks because I tried to predict anything.

Now, the regulars.

Top Team: Like I was saying, Ian’s team is looking pretty strong lately. Andrew Luck got off to a slow start, but hasn’t had a sub-20 point week since week 3 (and he’s only QB8?). Barkley is Barkley, but was actually a bit less Barkley than usual vs the Niners. Howard… Keenan Allen was solid, and has been trending up after a slow start forced his previous owner to ditch him in a stupid trade (won’t name names). Larry Fitzgerald is back in a starting lineup for a week until Adam Thielen returns. But the two best players this week were Zach Ertz, who was unguardable in the second half vs. the Cowboys, and Aaron Jones. Remember what I said about never trusting a Green Bay backfield? Guess I was wrong, because Aaron Jones is the guy in Green Bay, and he was a monster vs. Miami. Another thing I always say is that you don’t have 30 point games on accident if you’re a running back, but with the Packers, it’s tough to say if Aaron Jones will continue to be a focal point of an offense that has Aaron Rodgers in it. Jones is of course a solid start going forward, however.

Highest-Scoring Loser: Good thing I wasn’t playing Reece this week. Dude would have beaten everyone except Ian and the guy he played against, Jaiden. Talk about bad luck. But what’s not bad luck is the trade that he made with Damian during experience week. Do not give Reece your players, because they instantly turn into studs as soon as you sell them. Such was the case with Christian McCaffrey, the scrappy lunch-pail coach’s son with a high motor and a great football IQ. Gym rats, I’m out of white athlete euphemisms. Dude put up almost 35 last week which is the best he’s done all season. Probably ever.

Lowest-Scoring Winner: I bet it’s me, I bet it’s me… IT’S NOT ME! It’s Dame, and god dameit did he need a win. Go ahead, groan, but Damian’s second victory of the season would have still beat just about every other loser (except Reece of course). So he’s not really that low scoring of a winner. I mean, he eclipsed the century mark this week! So yeah, you traded away your best player and got shit in return, but at least you won! Who needs playoffs, anyways… :sadcat:

Bottom of the Barrel: Oh, how the mighty are not mighty anymore. That’s the phrase, right? Thomas didn’t start a kicker this week, so he’s clearly not paying attention anymore. I may have to set his lineups going forward. But even if he did start Chris Boswell, his team is just the worst this year. His best player was Ryan Fitzpatrick, who scored 13.7. None of his players eclipsed 15 points, and only three made it to double digits. Rough year for the former champ.

Best Player: Jaiden’s namesake, Big Ben Roethlisberger, did some uncouth things to the Panthers defense on Thursday night. Without even waiting, he took his chances and dove right in, scoring not once, not twice, but five times that night. But look, you might say – the Panthers were asking for it. Who just leaves their corners exposed like that? At least cover them up with a safety. Perhaps Cam Newton’s decision making was impaired. At any rate, that’s no excuse for the behavior that Ben exhibited. No “no” doesn’t mean “yes.”

…

Was that poor taste? I’m sorry.

Worst Start: Said Panthers D that got raped by Big Ben. RIP Justin.

Worst Bench: Thomas’s kicker wasn’t a good bench. Set your lineups, please. But we’re gonna go with Allen Robinson (Reece). If Reece had played the WR1 of the Bears instead of the WR…3? of the Packers, he would have beaten Jaiden. Oh well, Robinson was coming off of an injury, and it’s tough to start someone the same week that happens.

Weekly Challenge: This week’s challenge was to outperform your projections! So, $5 goes to Ian, who was projected for 113.2 but scored 141.8, a difference of 28.6! Nice going, Ian.
